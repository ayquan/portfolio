Analyzing the Effect of NAT on Nicotiana quadrivalvis Root Growth

Boyce Thompson Institute - Cornell University, Fall 2024

Drought tolerance is an important trait to study in plants. N-nitrosoanatabine(NAT) is hypothesized to be a key contributer to the drought resistance of Nicotiana quadrivalvis(tobacco) plants. This project analyzed the growth rate of tobacco roots at various concentrations of NAT agar solution.

Tobacco seedlings were imaged four days after being planted. Plates were imaged every day for ten days. The first image contains seedlings in 0mM NAT, the second image contains seedlings in 5mM NAT.

The first image contains seedlings in 0mM NAT, the second image contains seedlings in 5mM NAT after ten days. Clearly, NAT is effecting the root growth of these seedlings.

Concentrations of NAT below 1mM did not signifigantly redctuce root growth rates. However, past 1mM, NAT very signifigantly effected the seedlings. At 5mM NAT, there was very little root growth, and the roots began to curl instead of grow vertically.
